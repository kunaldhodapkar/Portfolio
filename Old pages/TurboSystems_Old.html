The Buzzword

As enterprises aggressively focus on digitization, cloud-computing continues to drive innovation for organizations.

In fact cloud apps have become so pervasive that 90% of businesses have adopted “cloud-based” approach.

In this era of cloud-computing, how can we leverage the power of cloud to empower enterprises?

Consider an example

Imagine, your microwave breaks down!

You call the service-center, the service agent takes down your details. Next day a technician magically shows up, fixes the microwave and it's working again. Pretty simple, right? Or not...

Behind-the-scenes

2 main systems come into scene when we talk enterprises

The service agent uses a CRM system to create a work order. They are esentially data sources.

Enterprises store critical business data in these systems. Over the years, CRM systems accumulate humungous amounts of business data.


Technicians use an enterprise-app to complete his daily work-orders and tasks.

These apps are often built on top of CRM systems to meet the business needs. Enterprises either develop their apps or use pre-built third party apps.


Early discussions with stakeholders helped us understand the background systems involved.

...when we understood the systems architecting customer experience, it revealed some major pain-points for enterprises

The sweet spots

Pain-points for enterprises

Massive IT infrastructural cost

Large enterprises host a dedicated IT team to build apps. Small, medium sized businesses often end up using spreadsheets.

Tediously long development cycles

Enterprise apps are developed by external vendor or internal IT team. Development cycles are long and tedious.

Locked in CRM systems

Data silos of enterprises fill up pretty quickly. Data migrations across CRM systems are often too risky or impossible.

All this means, the enterprises end up losing money and employees their productivity.

Introducing Turbo

World’s first cloud platform to rapidly build gorgeous enterprise apps in minutes, without writing a single line of code.

It seamlessly integrates with 140+ CRM systems so no need for data migration. The data stays safe where it is.

Overcoming Challenges

The project started on a haphazard note. However, as we proceeded in the project it was important for us to figure-out a strategy which worked for everyone.


Switching work location

For initial days, we opted to work at the client location. It made knowledge transfer much more efficient. Plus, it was important for us to work closely with the development team to understand technical constraints to produce feasible designs.


Lean UX to the rescue

Given the fast-paced nature of the project we decided to adopt lean UX to deliver designs in bi-weekly sprints. We chucked the entire platform into smaller modules & focussed on understanding in-detail only on one module at a time.

2 user segments, 2 user experiences

Weekly meetings with the stakeholders helped us understand the 2 distict audiences for the platform.

What goes on behind-the-scenes?

Businesses store critical data in "systems of record", and...

Businesses have come a long way from manual bookkeeping of business records to local storage in computer hard-drives. Today they store their critical business data (finance, HR, customer) in cloud platforms such as Salesforce, SAP, Mendix etc (called, Systems of record).


These "systems" are (super-) hard for regular employees to use, so...

However, as much as these Systems of record are valued for their ability to generate reports and insight for the company’s management team, they still remain complicated for the workers who engage with the customers on a regular basis, and often require years of expertise.


Businesses build custom workflow apps, which require IT support & money...

Due to complexity of Systems of record, businesses often have to develop their own workflow applications which are poor in usability and incur high developmental costs and long maintenance and deployment cycles.


Thus, they need a platform to build custom apps for employees (super-) fast

There is a dire need of a platform which integrates itself with different Systems of record, helps businesses to create custom workflow apps rapidly and provides a seamless experience to company workers to engage with the customers effectively.

Knowing our users

Detailed discussions with clients helped us come up with 2 distinct user-personas


App admins

They have critical role of keeping the applications in the organisation running. They install, update, diagnose, and support applications acquired either from a third-party vendor or from an internal development team.

Field workers

They are the users of these workflow applications. They interact with customers and internal stakeholders on a daily basis. They require these apps to be high in usability and rely on them to get their job done.

Mapping the Personas

Key takeaways

  • Remains on-field most of the time.
  • Connectivity is sporadic, goes offline anytime.
  • Collaborates with other technicians regularly.

Key takeaways

  • Collaborates with end-users to resolve issues.
